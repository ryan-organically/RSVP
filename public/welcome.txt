Welcome to RSVP Reader

You are reading this one word at a time. That is the whole idea.

RSVP stands for Rapid Serial Visual Presentation. Instead of dragging your eyes across lines of text, the words come to you, flashed in the same spot, one after another. Your eyes stop moving. The little jumps your eyes normally make between words, called saccades, disappear. What is left is just recognition, and recognition is fast.

Most people read somewhere between two hundred and three hundred words a minute on paper. With a little practice, RSVP can comfortably take you past four hundred, and many readers settle in around five or six hundred without losing the thread. You are in control of the pace. Slow down for the hard parts. Speed up when the going is easy.

A few things worth knowing.

The colored letter in each word is the focus point. It is positioned where your eye naturally lands when it recognizes a word, slightly left of center. Keep your gaze there and let everything else blur. You do not need to look at every letter. Your brain fills in the rest.

Press the space bar to start and stop. The left and right arrow keys nudge you backward and forward. The up and down arrows change your speed. Press B to drop a bookmark anywhere, and H to save a highlight you want to keep. Press the question mark key at any time to see the full list of shortcuts.

When you pause, the reader remembers exactly where you stopped, even if you close the tab and come back tomorrow. Everything you read stays on your own device. Nothing is uploaded anywhere. Your library is yours alone.

You can read almost anything here. Upload a text or PDF file. Paste an article straight from the web. Or browse the Free Library, where more than sixty thousand public-domain books are a single tap away, from Jane Austen to Marcus Aurelius.

If you ever feel lost in a long passage, that is normal at first. Drop your speed by fifty or a hundred words a minute and let yourself catch up. Comprehension comes back quickly once your eyes learn to stay still. Within a few sessions, the pace that felt frantic today will feel slow.

Speed is not the only point. The real gift of reading this way is attention. There is only one word in front of you, so there is nothing else to drift toward. No half-read paragraphs. No rereading the same line three times. Just you and the next word, and the next, and the next.

That is enough to get started. Pick something from your library and go. Read something good.
